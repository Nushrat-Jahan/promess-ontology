Introduction back to ToC

The PROMESS ontology is being developed to organise and document the main concepts involved in the study domain addressed by the project. It focuses on the participant pathway throughout the study, including inclusion and grouping, follow-up visits, clinical and biological assessment, exposure information, imaging examinations, temporality, treatments, and clinical outcomes.

The ontology has two complementary purposes. First, it provides a shared conceptual reference for researchers and collaborators who need a consistent understanding of the data, the terminology, and the relations between the main entities described in the project. Second, it provides the basis for a formal OWL implementation in which classes, properties, domains, ranges, cardinalities, and documentation metadata can be defined in a clear and reusable manner.

This documentation follows the usual structure adopted for ontology publication on the Web. Its purpose is to provide a readable and stable entry point to the ontology by presenting its metadata, its overall structure, and the descriptions of its classes and properties generated from the ontology itself.
