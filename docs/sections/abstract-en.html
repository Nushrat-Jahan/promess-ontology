Abstract

PROMESS is a draft ontology developed to support the structured representation and documentation of participant follow-up data in a clinical research context. Its current scope covers the study setting, participant groups, visits, medical conditions, risk factors, exposures, measurements, biological sampling, imaging examinations, temporality, treatments, and clinical results. Particular attention is given to the representation of longitudinal follow-up and to the distinction between exposures, clinical observations, biological measurements, and outcome assessment. The ontology is intended to provide a coherent and explicit vocabulary for the PROMESS domain, support its later formalisation in OWL, and facilitate shared understanding between domain experts and ontology engineers through clear and stable documentation.
