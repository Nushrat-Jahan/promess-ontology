Abstract

PROMESS is an ontology intended to support the structured representation of participant follow-up data in a clinical research setting. The current conceptual scope covers the study context, participant groups, visits, medical conditions, risk factors, exposures, measurements, biological sampling, imaging examinations, temporality, treatments, and clinical results. Particular attention is given to the representation of longitudinal follow-up and to the distinction between exposures, clinical observations, biological measurements, and outcome assessment.

The ontology is designed to provide a coherent vocabulary for describing the PROMESS domain, to make the underlying concepts explicit, and to support later formalization in OWL. It is also intended to facilitate communication between domain experts and ontology engineers by offering a clear, stable documentation layer that can be shared and reviewed.
